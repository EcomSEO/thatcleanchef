# ThatCleanChef — Competitive Landscape Analysis

**Prepared:** April 2026
**Scope:** SERP analysis + recipe-blog competitor landscape + 10 positioning moves.
**Target audience:** "Kate, 34, busy professional who cooks" + "Mike, 41, busy dad" + "Sam, 48, post-diagnosis."
**Source data:** DataForSEO April 2026 via `docs/thatcleanchef-research.md` + live SERP observations.

**Headline finding:** The recipe-blog space is one of the most crowded on the internet, but the keyword data shows clear pockets of opportunity — Whole30 recipes (KD 6), anti-inflammatory (KD 5), PCOS recipes (KD 9), cottage cheese trending wave (KD 18-22 at +240% to +620% YoY). The wedge is *editorial quality* (time-honesty, Nutrition Ledger, chef-authoritative voice) in a space where most content is scraped-AllRecipes, Pinterest clickbait, or endless food-blog preambles. ThatCleanChef's strategy: saturate the Ingredient × Diet × Meal-Type matrix with world-class photography + Recipe schema + honest prep times.

## Part 1 — SERP analysis

### 1.1 "Anti-inflammatory recipes" (18,100 vol / KD 5) — the easy win

**Live top 10:** EatingWell, AllRecipes, Pinch of Yum, Ambitious Kitchen, Pinterest cluster, Healthline, PureWow, TasteOfHome, Self, Reddit r/AntiInflammatoryDiet.

**Structural gap:** Only EatingWell attempts an "anti-inflammatory badge" system. NONE have a Nutrition Ledger per recipe. Most top results are recipe roundups (10-25 recipes per post) rather than individual chef-tested recipes.

**Our wedge:** individual chef-tested recipes with Nutrition Ledgers + a hub pillar that organizes them into a real meal plan. KD 5 means top-3 rank is achievable in 6-9 months.

### 1.2 "Whole30 recipes" (27,100 vol / KD 6)

**Live top 10:** Whole30.com (official), Well Plated, Paleo Running Momma, Nom Nom Paleo, AllRecipes, Pinch of Yum, Foodie Crush, The Real Food Dietitians.

**Structural gap:** A few sites (The Real Food Dietitians) do this well with proper Whole30 compliance checks, but most top results are scattered individual recipes without a proper "Wave 1 of Whole30" pillar. Our wedge: a Whole30 pillar that's honest about week 1-4 difficulty + 7 recipes per week.

### 1.3 "Mediterranean diet recipes" (33,100 vol / KD 18)

**Live top 10:** EatingWell (dominant), Mayo Clinic, Mediterranean Dish (small blog), AllRecipes, Food Network, various others.

**Structural gap:** EatingWell wins but is formulaic. Mediterranean Dish is good but small. Our wedge: chef-tested recipes (not just "Mediterranean-adjacent" labeled) + proper 7-day meal plan with Sunday prep sequence.

### 1.4 "High-protein breakfast" (49,500 vol / KD 11) — highest volume Wave 1 target

**Live top 10:** EatingWell, Forkly, The Girl On Bloor, Skinnytaste, Fit Foodie Finds, Live Eat Learn, various.

**Structural gap:** Every top-10 result lists 15-30 breakfasts without nutritional rigor. None commit to "45g protein minimum" as a criteria. Our wedge: fewer recipes, each hitting 40g+ with real Nutrition Ledger.

**Realistic ranking:** top-5 in 6-9 months on the broader term; top-3 on specific variants ("40g protein breakfast," "savory high-protein breakfast") within 90 days.

### 1.5 Cottage cheese trending wave (multiple keywords, KD 14-22)

- "cottage cheese recipes" — 40,500 vol / KD 18, **+240% YoY**
- "cottage cheese flatbread" — 2,900 vol / KD 22, **+620% YoY**
- "cottage cheese ice cream" — 8,100 vol / KD 18
- "cottage cheese pancakes" — 4,400 vol / KD 16

**This is the single fastest-growing search wave in 2026.** SERPs are less mature because the search volume is exploding faster than established blogs can publish. Fit Foodie Finds, Eating Bird Food, The Big Man's World captured the early wave but there's room for a new entrant doing the recipes better (chef-tested + Nutrition Ledger + better photography).

**Strategy:** 7 cottage cheese recipes in Wave 1 + a hub pillar linking them all. "Cottage Cheese Collection" becomes a defensible cluster.

### 1.6 "PCOS recipes" (3,600 vol / KD 9)

**Live top 10:** A few specialized PCOS sites (The PCOS Kitchen, PCOS Diet Support) + generic health sites (Healthline, EatingWell).

**Structural gap:** Small specialized sites lack editorial rigor; generic sites don't go deep on PCOS-specific considerations (insulin sensitivity, inflammation pattern). Our wedge: chef-tested recipes that also meet PCOS-specific criteria (moderate-GI carbs, adequate protein, anti-inflammatory ingredients).

**Easy rank:** KD 9 is very accessible. Top-3 in 60-90 days.

### 1.7 "Low-FODMAP recipes" (12,100 vol / KD 14)

Medical-nutrition niche. Monash University is the authority (they invented the FODMAP classification). Top 10 has a few dedicated low-FODMAP blogs.

**Our wedge:** we approach low-FODMAP as a *compatibility tag* on otherwise good recipes, not as a diet-cult identity. Recipes that happen to be low-FODMAP-friendly without sacrificing flavor.

## Part 3 — 10 positioning moves

### 1. Nutrition Ledger on every recipe
USDA FDC-backed macros on every recipe, visible above the fold, not buried in a modal. This single differentiator is worth 40% of our editorial moat — nobody else in the top 10 for most SERPs does this consistently.

### 2. Time honesty (prep + dishes included)
"45 minutes including dishes" beats "15 minutes" every time once the reader discovers we're honest. Time-honest recipes get better reviews, higher returns, and genuine word-of-mouth.

### 3. Recipe schema engineered end-to-end
Every recipe has complete Recipe schema with nutrition, multiple image sizes, step-by-step instructions with images. Validation runs in CI. Google's recipe SERP features reward this mechanically.

### 4. Original photography (batch-shot quarterly)
No stock. Every recipe has 4+ original images. Photography is the largest cost at launch ($8-15k for Wave 1 photographer + stylist) but it's the moat that AllRecipes can't match.

### 5. Pinterest-tall image on every recipe
1000×1500 per recipe. Pinterest is a primary distribution channel — treating pin design as a first-class design concern (headline overlay, brand-consistent fonts) is table stakes.

### 6. Ingredient × Diet × Meal-Type matrix saturation
Cottage cheese flatbread is tagged: High-Protein + Mediterranean-adjacent + Vegetarian + Breakfast + 20-Minute. One recipe, 5 discovery paths. The matrix approach maximizes internal-linking opportunity.

### 7. Chef notes section on every recipe
"Why this works" with 3-5 technique decisions explained. This is the differentiator vs AllRecipes-tier content. Readers who cook will notice.

### 8. Cottage cheese wave fast-publishing
+620% YoY on cottage cheese flatbread is the kind of search wave that rewards speed. 7 cottage cheese recipes in Wave 1 captures this rising tide before competitors can publish.

### 9. Display ads at 50k sessions (Mediavine)
Accepted commercial reality of recipe sites. Careful placement (never between ingredients, never between steps). Unlocks $2-5k/mo in revenue that pure affiliate cannot.

### 10. Lead-magnet-driven email list
The "Clean Chef Starter Kit" 14-day meal plan PDF drives email captures at high rates. Email is the distribution channel Mediavine doesn't touch — compounds over time, converts well on cookbook PDF + membership products (post-month-8).

## Part 4 — Risks

### Competitive
- **AllRecipes / EatingWell launches Nutrition Ledger equivalent.** Low probability but possible. Mitigation: combine with chef notes + time honesty + photography — structurally harder to copy.
- **Another chef-led clean-eating site launches.** Possible. Mitigation: speed + photographer retention + Pinterest head-start.
- **TikTok creator with recipes + cookbook crosses over to SEO.** Already happening (Cjeatsrecipes). Mitigation: focus on Google search + Pinterest, not TikTok. Different distribution.

### Platform
- **Google recipe SERP algorithm update.** Favors complete Recipe schema + rich photography + fast LCP. All factors we already optimize.
- **Pinterest algorithm change.** Main risk — Pinterest traffic can shift dramatically. Mitigation: diversify email + Google Discover + Google organic.
- **Mediavine acquisition / rate change.** Possible. Mitigation: Raptive alternative prepared for month 9+.

### Content
- **Recipe failures at scale.** A single recipe that doesn't work generates low-trust reviews. Mitigation: test every recipe 2+ times, including with 1 non-chef tester.
- **Nutritional calculation errors.** USDA FDC is the canonical source, but calculation errors happen. Mitigation: automated calculation via Edamam or USDA API with manual spot-checks; dated revision notes when corrected.
- **Photography bottleneck.** Photographer availability, weather for natural light. Mitigation: 6-month photographer contract + studio lighting as backup.

### Legal
- **Recipe copyright challenge.** Recipes aren't copyrightable, but execution + photography is. Mitigation: original recipe development + original photography.
- **Nutritional health claims.** Anti-inflammatory framing is evidence-backed but could be scrutinized. Mitigation: cite the literature on specific ingredients (turmeric, omega-3) + disclaimer on medical claims.

## Bottom line

ThatCleanChef competes in the most crowded SERP in the network — but has the clearest path to $6-8k/mo revenue because recipe sites monetize well (display + affiliate + digital products + cookbook future).

The three compounding moats:
1. Editorial quality (Nutrition Ledger, time honesty, chef notes) — hard to copy retroactively
2. Original photography archive — expensive for competitors to replicate
3. Email list + lead magnet — compounds over years

Biggest risks: photography bottleneck + Pinterest algorithm. Mitigations: 6-month photographer retention + diversified distribution.

Biggest opportunity: cottage cheese wave (+240% to +620% YoY). Publish fast in Wave 1. The search volume is exploding faster than established food blogs can adapt.
