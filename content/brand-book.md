# ThatCleanChef — Brand Book

Voice, audience, and visual identity for thatcleanchef.com — clean-eating recipes with chef-authoritative technique notes and a Nutrition Ledger on every recipe.

**Source research:** `docs/thatcleanchef-research.md` (DataForSEO April 2026).

**Important deviation from CLAUDE.md:** The original plan placed ThatCleanChef as Wave 4 "awaiting chef hire." Research repositions this as a Wave 4-buildable site where the **chef-authoritative voice** is achievable via editorial rigor (tested recipes, chef technique notes, proper photography) without requiring a named chef hire at launch. Named chef can be added later as a growth upgrade.

---

## 1. Who we are

ThatCleanChef is a recipe site that respects your time.

We publish recipes that actually work. We test them in home kitchens on home equipment. We time them honestly — 45 minutes including dishes, not 15 minutes of "active prep" hiding a 30-minute stock reduction. We include a Nutrition Ledger with USDA-sourced macros on every recipe because macros matter for the people cooking these dinners.

We are not a food blog with a life story before the recipe. We are not an AllRecipes-style firehose of user uploads. We are the site that puts "45 minutes, 4 servings, 38g protein" at the top, the recipe in the second screen, and the chef technique notes at the bottom — in that order.

We're diet-inclusive (Anti-Inflammatory, Whole30, Mediterranean, PCOS, Low-FODMAP, High-Protein) but we're not diet-scolding. No "guilt-free." No "you-deserve-this." Just clean plates and sharp recipes.

## 2. The reader

### Primary persona — "Kate, 34, busy professional who actually cooks"

Product manager, consultant, or similar knowledge worker. $130-200k. Lives in a mid-to-large US city. Cooks 4-6 dinners a week because she likes it and because takeout every night is $900/month and not as good. Has a decent kitchen (Le Creuset Dutch oven, sharp knives, Vitamix, a kitchen scale she uses occasionally).

She's tired of:
- Pinterest clickbait recipes that don't work (salt, acid, fat off-balance)
- 2,500-word food blog preambles about the author's grandmother
- "30-minute meals" that take 90 minutes including prep
- Vegan-mandatory or gluten-free-mandatory recipes when she just wants a good dinner
- Nutrition info being either absent or wildly inaccurate

She follows Alison Roman, Sohla El-Waylly, Brian Lagerstrom, Andy Baraghani. She cooks from *Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat*. She bought a copy of *Six Seasons*. She watched Sohla's egg tutorials on YouTube.

She's sometimes trying a specific pattern — post-40, her doctor mentioned inflammation; post-gym, she wants more protein; post-baby, the whole family is navigating PCOS — but she doesn't want her dinners to feel diet-restrictive. She wants a good recipe that happens to fit her current pattern.

### Secondary persona — "Mike, 41, busy dad"

Tuesday night: got home at 6:47, kids need food by 7:30, Mike needs high-protein because he trained at 5am. Wants: 30 minutes, sheet pan, 40g protein, something the 6-year-old will eat.

Mike arrives on the High-Protein hub. We serve him fast.

### Tertiary persona — "Sam, 48, post-diagnosis"

Just got labs back. Cholesterol higher than last year. Doctor said "Mediterranean-ish." Sam is looking for a real starting point — not a diet plan, a first week of dinners.

Sam lands on the Mediterranean pillar + the 7-day meal plan.

### We write for Kate by default

Mike and Sam come along because recipes that work for Kate work for them too.

## 3. The promise

> Clean plates, sharp recipes.

Three things every ThatCleanChef recipe must deliver:

1. **Time honesty.** "45 minutes including dishes" — not "15 min prep + 30 min cook." If the stock takes 20 minutes to reduce while you do nothing, that's 20 minutes.
2. **Nutrition Ledger.** USDA FDC-backed: calories, protein, fiber, sodium, saturated fat, added sugar. Per serving. Highlighted macros for the recipe's target audience (protein for high-protein recipes; fiber for Mediterranean).
3. **Why this works.** Every recipe has a chef-notes section explaining the technique choices. Why sear then braise. Why salt the chicken 30 min before. Why the acid goes in at the end. Not showy — just the 3-5 decisions that make the recipe better than the 8 similar versions on AllRecipes.

## 4. Voice

### How we sound
- **Chef-authoritative.** Confident in the technique. "Do this, not that, because X."
- **Time-honest.** No hidden prep, no assumed stock-on-hand, no "simply reduce the sauce."
- **Specific.** "Kosher salt" not "salt." "High-smoke-point fat (avocado or refined olive)" not "oil." "10-inch skillet" not "a pan."
- **Diet-inclusive not diet-mandated.** A recipe can be both Whole30 and Mediterranean; we tag both.
- **Warm without being performative.** We care about the food. We don't need to tell you a story about our grandmother.
- **Alison-Roman-adjacent.** Confident voice, good food, not trying too hard.

### How we don't sound
- ❌ Food blog preamble ("Growing up, my grandmother always made this for Sunday dinner, and the smell of garlic roasting would fill the whole house...")
- ❌ Diet-scold ("Ditch the sugar and reclaim your health!")
- ❌ Clickbait ("This recipe changed my life — you HAVE to try it!")
- ❌ Hack-coded ("The secret no one tells you about cooking chicken")
- ❌ Guilt-free / skinny / slimming / thin — ever
- ❌ Mommy-blog ("Hey mamas! Today I'm sharing a recipe the whole fam loves!")
- ❌ Instagram-caption ("obsessing over this bowl right now 😍✨")

### Side-by-side examples

**❌ Don't write this:**
> OMG you guys, this anti-inflammatory golden chicken soup is literally EVERYTHING right now. I've been making it all winter and it's the perfect cozy meal! It's packed with so many healing ingredients and it's totally guilt-free. You HAVE to make this!

**✅ Write this:**
> Golden chicken soup is what you make when you want something warm that also delivers. The turmeric and ginger aren't for wellness points — they're in because they make a chicken broth taste better and add color. Bone-in thighs give you more collagen and more flavor than breasts. 40 minutes active, 4 servings, 32g protein, 580 calories. Served with rice or without, both shown below.

---

**❌ Don't write this:**
> This 30-minute dinner is so easy and delicious! Simply sauté the chicken, add the vegetables, and simmer until done. Serve immediately and enjoy!

**✅ Write this:**
> 45 minutes including dishes. Sear chicken thighs in one pan while rice cooks in another (start both at 0:00). At 8 minutes in, pull chicken, add shallot + garlic + ginger to the same pan, deglaze with soy. Return chicken, add broth, simmer 12 minutes. Rice finishes around the 25-minute mark; let it rest 5. Thighs hit 165°F around the 30-minute mark. Plate, add the acid (lime) last. Total: 45. Dishes: 2 pans + a cutting board.

## 5. Visual identity

### Colors
- **Primary**: Sage herb `#7A8F6B` (kitchen-herb green — dill, parsley, sage)
- **Accent**: Warm cream `#F6F1E8` (kitchen paper, linen napkin)
- **Highlight**: Kitchen terracotta `#C4663D` (Le Creuset, copper pot, warm accent)
- **Deep**: Olive `#4A5530` (cast iron, darker kitchen)
- **Text**: Charcoal `#2B2B2B` on cream

### Typography
- **Headlines**: **Fraunces** or **Source Serif 4** — editorial, warm, food-magazine-adjacent
- **Body**: **Inter** at 17px, line-height 1.65
- **Recipe ingredients**: Monospace for measurements, sans for ingredient names
- **Nutrition Ledger**: **IBM Plex Mono** for numerics
- **Chef notes**: Italicized serif, slightly smaller, amber-colored leader word

### Photography style — the primary visual
Photography IS the product. Every recipe has:

1. **Hero photo** — overhead or 3/4 angle, natural light, real styling
2. **Process shot** (1-3) — key technique moments (the sear, the plate assembly, the slice)
3. **Final beauty shot** — eye-level, plated, ready-to-eat
4. **Pinterest-tall shot** — 1000×1500, clear headline overlay, saves well

Quality bar:
- Natural light only (no studio flash)
- Real food, real cooking (not styled plastic)
- Consistent color palette across the shoot (muted, warm, kitchen-real)
- No Instagram filters
- No food-blog clichés (wooden spoon sprinkled with herbs, burlap everywhere)

Batching: 10 recipes per 2-day studio shoot. Pre-shoot prep list, shot list, brand color guide distributed to photographer ahead of time.

### What our pages look like
- Warm cream background
- Hero photo fills first screen
- Recipe card prominent (time, servings, Nutrition Ledger, jump-to-recipe button for people who want to skip the intro)
- 3-paragraph intro max — then the recipe
- Chef notes at the end
- Nutrition Ledger always visible (not buried in modal)
- Print button, scale button (2×, 4×), "copy ingredients to shopping list" button
- Pinterest pin button
- Display ads allowed (see editorial principle #10) — placed thoughtfully, not within recipe steps

### What we don't look like
- AllRecipes (dense, ad-heavy, bad photography, low quality control)
- Food blog (rambling preamble, life stories, "jump to recipe" hidden)
- Instagram (filter-heavy, styled for social not cooking)
- Diet marketing site ("slim down," "flat belly," before/after implied)
- Chef-memoir blog (recipes as vehicles for autobiography)

## 6. Editorial principles (thatcleanchef-specific, on top of network)

1. **Nutrition Ledger on every recipe.** USDA FDC-sourced. Calories, protein, fiber, sodium, saturated fat, added sugar. Per serving. Non-negotiable.

2. **Time honesty.** All times include prep dishes, prep cleanup, and waiting periods. No "15 minutes" that's actually 45.

3. **Recipe schema on every recipe.** Full: `recipeIngredient`, `recipeInstructions`, `nutrition`, `cookTime`, `prepTime`, `totalTime`, `recipeYield`, `recipeCategory`, `recipeCuisine`, `keywords`, `image` (array with multiple sizes), `aggregateRating` (once reviews exist).

4. **Chef notes section.** Every recipe has a "Why this works" block with 3-5 technique decisions explained. This is the differentiator vs AllRecipes.

5. **Original photography.** No stock. Ever. Recipes without real photography don't ship.

6. **Diet tags, not diet dogma.** We tag recipes by diet compatibility (Anti-Inflammatory, Whole30, Mediterranean, PCOS, Low-FODMAP, Paleo, Keto, Vegetarian, Vegan-friendly). We don't scold readers for eating outside a diet.

7. **No "guilt-free," "skinny," "slimming," "thin," "flat belly," or diet-culture language.** Ever.

8. **Claims are cited.** "Anti-inflammatory" is not a marketing claim; it's backed by ingredient evidence (turmeric, ginger, fatty fish — cited to research). PCOS-friendly recipes cite the insulin-sensitivity literature.

9. **Honest test footprint.** "Tested 3 times in a 10-inch carbon steel skillet on a gas range" is acceptable disclosure. We say what equipment and how many times.

10. **Display ads allowed (network exception).** Recipe sites monetize via Mediavine/Raptive display at $18-30 RPM at 50k+ sessions. This is the economic exception in the network. Ad placement is thoughtful — never within recipe steps, never between ingredients, never between a photo and its caption.

## 9. Reader journey

1. **Reader Googles** — "high protein breakfast," "anti-inflammatory dinner," "cottage cheese recipes," "30 minute chicken"
2. **Lands on a recipe** — hero photo + time + Nutrition Ledger in the first screen
3. **Jumps to recipe** (via button) OR reads the 3-paragraph intro — both paths respected
4. **Saves to Pinterest** OR prints the recipe OR adds to shopping list
5. **Subscribes** to the "Clean Chef Starter Kit" (14-day anti-inflammatory meal plan PDF — primary lead magnet)
6. **Returns** for weekly meal plans, seasonal content, the recipe library search
7. **Long-term:** buys the cookbook PDF ($19) or subscribes to the weekly meal plan membership ($5/mo)

Lead magnet Wave 1: **"Clean Chef Starter Kit"** — 14-day anti-inflammatory meal plan PDF with grocery list, macros totaled per day, prep-ahead checklist. Free, Beehiiv-delivered.

## 10. Distribution strategy

- **Pinterest** — primary. Pinterest-tall (1000×1500) photo on every recipe. 3-5 pin variants per recipe. Pin to 8-10 active clean-eating / high-protein / Mediterranean group boards.
- **Google search** — dominant for long-tail recipes ("cottage cheese flatbread," "anti-inflammatory breakfast bowl"). Recipe schema + fast LCP win here.
- **Google Discover** — the seasonal-recipe traffic source. Requires fast Core Web Vitals + strong Pinterest-tall image.
- **Newsletter** — weekly with one featured recipe + seasonal drop.
- **Instagram (later)** — secondary. Recipe hooks, not recipes. Drive to site for the actual content.

## 12. The line we never cross

ThatCleanChef exists because the recipe-blog space is a mess — ads stuffed between every ingredient, 2,500-word preambles, recipes that don't work. The moment we become another one of those sites, the reason to come back disappears.

Specifically:
- We do not publish recipes we haven't tested
- We do not publish recipes without original photography
- We do not place display ads between a recipe's ingredients
- We do not place display ads within recipe steps
- We do not inflate prep times (lie backwards)
- We do not use diet-culture language
- We do not pretend to be a single-chef blog
- We do not accept sponsored posts without clear labeling
- We do not let affiliate revenue bias a recipe's ingredient recommendations

The Nutrition Ledger + time-honesty + original photography + chef notes are the moat. They compound over years.
